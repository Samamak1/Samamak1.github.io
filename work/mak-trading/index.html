Context & stakes

Growth was only valuable if the operating cadence could retain it.

A founder-led education community must repeatedly earn trust while coordinating acquisition, live content, member support, access control, payment, moderation, and privacy.

The community began in 2019 and added paid membership primarily during 2022–2023. I led the program across brand, product design, curriculum, acquisition, member journey, content operations, and the supporting platform stack.

Business objective

Create a trusted education product with a broad free community, a distinct paid experience, and systems that could support recurring delivery.

Program constraints

  • Three-person founding team
  • Founder-dependent content capacity
  • Multiple external platforms
  • Payment and member privacy
  • Financial-education claim sensitivity
  • Migration and churn risk

Member lifecycle

Design the journey from attention to durable participation.

02 · OnboardRoles, channels, access, member orientation
03 · EducateCurriculum, live sessions, options and risk content
04 · EngageRecurring cadence, discussion, moderation
05 · MonetizeLaunchPass, Stripe, paid access, trial conversion
06 · RetainMember value, support, renewal, feedback

Critical decisions

Platform choices shaped the member experience.

01

Reach versus monetization

Maintained a broad community while creating a separate paid experience; total community size and paid-member counts were tracked and communicated distinctly.

02

Fast launch versus platform control

LaunchPass and Stripe enabled faster monetization. WordPress and MemberPress later offered more control, with greater migration and maintenance complexity.

03

Acquisition versus retention quality

Campaigns were paired with onboarding, recurring live programming, and an original curriculum so growth had an operating system behind it.

04

Promotional energy versus credibility

Positioned the product around education, risk management, process, and transparency—not investment advice or promises of returns.

Platform migration

Move the system without losing the member.

The program operated across multiple community and billing channels. LaunchPass enabled rapid paid access; Stripe handled recurring payments; WordPress and MemberPress later increased ownership and control. I coordinated access logic, payment continuity, member data, content structure, and member communication through the transition.

What I led

  • Community and product strategy
  • Brand and campaign direction
  • Member journey and content cadence
  • Original 48-page curriculum
  • Platform and migration decisions

What stayed protected

  • Member identities and contact details
  • Payment and account records
  • Brokerage or performance data
  • Private community discussions
  • Any investment-return claims

Reflection

Attention creates a launch. Cadence creates a community.

Acquisition opened the door. Trust, onboarding, recurring programming, and a clear member journey gave people a reason to stay.

The scaling constraint

Founder authenticity was a strength and eventually a capacity limit. The next stage would separate the highest-value founder moments from repeatable delivery that trained moderators and instructors could run without weakening the experience.
