Context & stakes

Every workstream had to converge at opening.

A menu is not ready if the price, POS button, ingredient, station, trained employee, vendor delivery, and service standard do not arrive together.

I was brought in to coordinate the launch and build the operating foundation. The concept combined a large tequila program, new systems, a newly recruited team, construction and facility dependencies, and high expected volume.

Business objective

Open a distinctive, high-volume concept with the operating controls, people, product, and systems to sustain service after launch.

My ownership

Launch roadmap, workstream coordination, operating design, vendor and system workflows, staffing and training, menu simplification, pricing, and readiness.

The original Monday.com roadmap remains private because even a redacted export retained a third-party account-setup task that was not relevant to this case. The workstream and dependency model above is the public, sanitized reconstruction.

Critical decisions

Simplification created capacity.

01

Menu breadth versus execution

Reduced a planned 45-item operating menu to 12 tapas dishes so a new kitchen could stabilize quality, training, inventory, and throughput.

02

Variety versus bar speed

Preserved the tequila-led identity while simplifying the cocktail workflow from approximately two minutes to about 30 seconds.

03

Manual judgment versus pricing consistency

Converted supplier invoices into a formula-driven cost and pricing system that made margin logic repeatable across products and POS entries.

04

Everything at launch versus the real critical path

Prioritized what was necessary to open, transact, schedule, train, stock, and serve; secondary enhancements moved behind launch readiness.

Readiness & execution

Make the handoffs visible before they become failures.

Control points

  • Named workstream owners and dates
  • Dependencies tied to soft opening
  • POS, menu, price, and inventory checks
  • Training and station-readiness sign-offs
  • Post-soft-opening feedback and correction

Operating infrastructure

  • Toast and Sling implementation
  • Supplier and purchasing workflows
  • $50K US Foods credit line
  • Floor, bar, and staffing design
  • Pricing and menu operating systems

Reflection

The launch date is a system-integration test.

A timeline shows when tasks are due. Program leadership reveals which handoff will prevent the guest experience from working—and resolves it before opening night.

What I carried forward

I now build readiness around scenarios, not task completion alone. “Configured” is different from “usable under load.” The strongest launch reviews test the full chain from customer demand to frontline execution and financial capture.
