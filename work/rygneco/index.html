Context & stakes

The software problem started in the warehouse.

An e-waste client does not only need a pickup. Months later, they may need to know what arrived, what was tested, where it went, how data was handled, what value was recovered, and what evidence supports the report.

The original workflow was fragmented across intake records, device condition, testing, disposition, resale, recycling, documentation, and client reporting. Before defining a platform, I helped run an enterprise healthcare pilot and used the field exceptions to expose the real requirements.

Business objective

Create an operating and product model that could move assets through multiple disposition paths while preserving traceability, financial logic, and client-ready reporting.

Program constraints

  • Limited MVP budget
  • Inconsistent source data
  • Mixed device conditions
  • Security and chain-of-custody needs
  • External contributors and evolving brand direction

Program architecture

One lifecycle, four stakeholder views.

I translated the physical lifecycle into product requirements, roles, exception paths, and evidence needs. The platform direction separated what each stakeholder needed to do from what every stakeholder needed to trust.

01 · Field operationsPickup, inventory, condition, testing, disposition
02 · ProductRequirements, portal workflows, roles, acceptance
03 · Value recoveryPricing inputs, refurb costs, resale and recycling paths
04 · AssuranceData-destruction records, chain of custody, exceptions
05 · ReportingFinancial, environmental, asset, and client summaries
06 · DeliveryVendor milestones, collaborator reviews, scope governance
The later vendor MVP architecture used React/Next.js, Node/Express, PostgreSQL on AWS, and separate client, operations, reseller, and administration experiences. Development was vendor- and collaborator-assisted; my responsibility was product and program direction.

Critical decisions

Every architecture choice was also an operating tradeoff.

01

External build versus internal capacity

A vendor accelerated delivery within the available budget. The tradeoff was greater dependence on milestone clarity, decision records, and acceptance criteria.

02

Broad vision versus focused MVP

Inventory, disposition, data-destruction records, pricing logic, and reporting were prioritized ahead of peripheral marketplace and engagement concepts.

03

Speed versus traceability

The workflow had to stay usable in the field while preserving enough evidence for a client to understand each asset’s path later.

04

Impact storytelling versus claim integrity

Reporting was framed around transparent methodology and evidence, not unsupported certification or carbon-credit claims.

Delivery & governance

Turn pilot learning into executable work.

The operating sequence became the program sequence: observe, define, prioritize, assign, review, accept, and learn.

I owned

  • Operating-problem definition
  • Lifecycle and portal requirements
  • Program priorities and scope decisions
  • Vendor direction and milestone review
  • Pricing, cost-model, and reporting concepts

Partners delivered

  • Vendor UI/UX and software implementation
  • Intern development contributions
  • AWS architectural advice
  • Legal, brand, and sustainability research
  • Operational input from collaborators

Program evidence

Make the operating model visible.

The vendor-built prototype made the program tangible: stakeholder roles, pickup flow, inventory, exception handling, finance, evidence, and reporting in one operating view.

Reflection

The field workflow was the real product specification.

Software did not solve the ambiguity; defining ownership at every physical handoff did. The strongest product decisions came from tracing exceptions and asking what evidence a client would need months later.

What I would strengthen next

Establish a tighter provenance matrix and acceptance checklist earlier, separating discovery prototypes, vendor-delivered features, operating evidence, and future-state concepts. That makes scope, authorship, and readiness clearer for every stakeholder.
