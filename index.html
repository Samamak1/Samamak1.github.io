The through-line

Operator first. Builder because of it.

I'm not a career software engineer. I'm the person who runs the shift, owns the P&L, signs the LLC paperwork — and then gets tired of doing the slow part by hand. Every system in my portfolio exists because I personally needed it to run something real: a bar that needed prices before Friday, a store that needed 43 people scheduled without breaking house rules, a community of paying members who needed content every single day.

I build with AI as a working partner — Cursor and Claude sit next to me the way a contractor's crew does. What makes the work mine is the part AI can't do: knowing the operation well enough to encode its real rules, and being accountable when the schedule posts or the price is wrong.

How I work

The rules I keep.

The operation is the spec

I don't build from imagined requirements. The 5-hour station cap, the no-close-then-open rule, the 4× pour markup — those came from running the floor, not from a brief.

Ship the boring artifact

The deliverable is never the code. It's the posted schedule, the priced menu, the report a client can hand their board. If the artifact doesn't work on paper, the system doesn't work.

Honesty is the moat
