How I lead programs

Structure the ambiguity. Mobilize the people. Govern the outcome.

I bring an operator’s accountability to program management: the plan must survive contact with the real workflow, and the team must be able to run it after handoff.

  1. 01

    Frame the business outcome

    Define why the work matters, who experiences the problem, the baseline, and what evidence will count as success.

  2. 02

    Map workstreams and dependencies

    Translate the objective into owners, milestones, constraints, critical handoffs, decision rights, and a realistic path to delivery.

  3. 03

    Align stakeholders around tradeoffs

    Surface competing priorities early, make decisions legible, and separate “must ship” from “valuable later.”

  4. 04

    Run a governance cadence

    Use a small set of KPIs, risks, decisions, and escalations to keep teams and executives working from the same truth.

  5. 05

    Build adoption into delivery

    Pair the product or process with training, documentation, reinforcement, and clear ownership—because launch is not adoption.

  6. 06

    Measure, learn, and institutionalize

    Compare outcome to baseline, close control gaps, document the learning, and make the improved behavior repeatable.
